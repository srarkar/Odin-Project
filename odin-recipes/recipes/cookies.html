Chocolate Chip Cookies

Home

Description

Chocolate chip cookies are the quintessential baked good, holding a permanent crown in the realm of sweet treats. A flawless balance of buttery, caramelized dough, pockets of melted chocolate, and a delicate hint of sea salt. To take a bite of a warm cookie is to experience pure bliss, triggering memories of cozy kitchens and a timeless appreciation for the simpler things in life.

From personal experience, chilling the cookie dough in the refrigerator for at least 24 hours drastically improves the depth of flavor and prevents excessive spreading.

Ingredients

  • 2 and 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups semi-sweet or dark chocolate chips (or chunks)
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • [Optional but recommended] Flaky sea salt for topping

Steps

  1. Cream butter and sugars: In a large bowl, beat the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar together until the mixture is light, fluffy, and thoroughly combined.
  2. Add wet ingredients: Beat in the eggs one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract, ensuring everything is fully incorporated into a smooth base.
  3. Combine dry ingredients: Whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt in a separate bowl, then slowly mix into the wet ingredients until a soft dough forms.
  4. Fold in chocolate: Gently stir in the chocolate chips or chunks by hand until they are evenly distributed throughout the dough. Chill the dough in the fridge for at least 1 hour.
  5. Scoop and bake: Scoop rounded portions of dough onto a parchment-lined baking sheet, leaving space between each. Bake at 375°F for 9–11 minutes until the edges are golden brown but the centers are still soft.
  6. Cool and serve: Remove from the oven and immediately sprinkle with flaky sea salt if desired. Let sit on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. (Makes 24–30 cookies).
